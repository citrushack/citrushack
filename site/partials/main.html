About us

Citrus Hack is the Inland Empire's premier hackathon — a 36 hour event for innovators, organized by students at the University of California, Riverside.

Established in 2015, Citrus Hack invites across California and throughout the world to cultivate new and exciting technologies. For our second annual hackathon, we aim to bring value to the technology sector through innovation from the next generation of developers.

Held again this year at the wonderful Winston Chung Hall at the University of California, Riverside.

Registration opens on August 29th! We hope to see you here.

What is a Hackathon?
A hackathon is a competition in which students team up to create projects. Hackathons have nothing to do with illegal security breaches. See this for a good explanation.
What kind of project can I make?
Mobile apps, websites, games, and hardware hacks are popular choices.
Am I eligible to come?
In order to attend you must be an undergraduate (or recent grad) student at an accredited school, and you must be at least 16 years of age. Unfortunately, we do NOT accept high school students at this time.
I don't have a team
You don't need to have a team per-se (although we encourage it). We will be having a small social before hacking starts so you can join/form a team if you don't already have one before coming.
Travel Reimbursements
We currently do not support any form of travel reimbursements; however, we are working towards adding this benefit.
Why do I have to apply?
We anticipate having a large number of students wanting to come to our event, and unfortunately we have a limit as to how many people we can physically fit in our venue. We will ensure there will be a good mix of beginner and veteran participants.
When will I know if I was accepted?
We will be sending out our first wave of acceptance emails towards the end of September. We will keep you informed.
